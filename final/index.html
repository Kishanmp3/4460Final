Understanding Unemployment in the UK

Trends, Disparities, and Insights

Unemployment is a critical measure of economic health and social stability. This project explores key patterns in unemployment across age groups, genders, regions, and time, while examining the role of government expenditure and economic resilience.

Unemployment Rate by Age Group

This visualization highlights how unemployment affects different age groups in the UK. Younger age groups, especially 16-24, consistently experience the highest rates of unemployment, reflecting challenges in job entry and economic vulnerability during crises.

Male vs Female Unemployment Rates

Gender dynamics add another layer of complexity to unemployment trends. Historically, male unemployment has spiked during economic crises, but the gap between genders has narrowed over time, reflecting shifting workforce participation.

Government Expenditure Breakdown

Government spending plays a pivotal role in addressing unemployment. This visualization shows how expenditures across sectors, such as health, education, and social security, shift during times of economic stress.

Employment and Unemployment Rates Over Time

Employment and unemployment have an inverse relationship. This graph highlights the peaks and troughs of both metrics, showcasing how external shocks like the 2008 financial crisis and COVID-19 impacted the UK job market.

Unemployment Rates Across UK Regions

Regional disparities in unemployment rates highlight the uneven economic recovery across the UK. Certain regions, such as the North and parts of Wales, consistently face higher unemployment, while the Southeast and East fare better.

Conclusion

Addressing unemployment requires targeted policies to support vulnerable age groups, bridge gender disparities, and address regional inequalities. By understanding these trends, we pave the way for a more inclusive economy and equitable recovery.
